1. Wear a Helmet

Always wear a properly fitted helmet to protect your head in case of accidents.

2. Obey Traffic Rules

Follow traffic signals, stop signs, and use hand signals when turning. Bicycles must obey the same rules as vehicles.

3. Stay Visible

Use lights and reflective gear, especially when riding at night or in low visibility conditions. Make sure drivers can see you.

4. Use Bike Lanes

Whenever possible, ride in designated bike lanes or on bike paths. This helps separate cyclists from motorized traffic.

5. Signal Your Intentions

Use hand signals to indicate when you're turning left, right, or stopping. This helps drivers and other cyclists know your intentions.

6. Check Your Equipment

Regularly inspect your bike to ensure it's in good working condition. Check brakes, tires, and lights if applicable.

7. Ride in the Same Direction as Traffic

Always ride on the right side of the road, in the same direction as traffic. Never ride against the flow of traffic.

8. Watch for Parked Cars

Be cautious when passing parked cars, as doors can suddenly open. Give parked cars enough space.

9. Avoid Distractions

Don't use your phone or wear headphones while cycling. Stay focused on the road and your surroundings.

10. Stay Alert

Pay attention to your surroundings. Watch for pedestrians, other cyclists, and unexpected obstacles.

11. Maintain a Safe Following Distance

Leave enough space between your bike and the vehicle in front of you to react to sudden stops or maneuvers. Maintain a safe following distance, especially at higher speeds.

12. Be Mindful of Weather Conditions

Adjust your riding style according to the weather. Wet or icy roads can reduce traction, so slow down and use caution when cycling in adverse weather conditions.
